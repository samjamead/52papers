52 Papers FAQs

Why did you start 52 Papers?

I have a fascination with the big issues in education, one of which is the research-practice gap. It was stimulated by a research project during my PGCE

Background story

Towards the end of my PGCE I became increasingly interested in the big issues in education. In particular, the issues that grabbed my attention at that time were teacher recruitment and retention; the research-practice gap; the attainment gap; and the attainment grouping of students.

At that point it seemed very difficult to see how I could contribute to the solutions of some of those problems. This sentiment was echoed by a colleage, who lamented that it seemed so hard to do anything to influence Whitehall.

After a weekend of reflection I decided that the best thing to do would be to just start doing something, anthing to get the wheels turning and see what came of it.

And so, 52 Papers was born: My first attempt at contributing to the big issues in education, by making a small bridge accross the research-practice gap.
